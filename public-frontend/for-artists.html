How it works

1

Sign up and import your catalog

Create an account, then import your tracks via CSV, search by name, or connect through your distributor. We use ISRCs to link everything.

2

Record or upload stories

Pick a song, tap record, and tell the story. Or upload a pre-recorded audio file. Stories can be 5 seconds to 5 minutes.

3

Review and publish

Listen back, add a transcript if you want, then hit publish. You can verify it to signal to platforms that it's artist-approved.

4

We handle distribution

Your story is packaged and delivered to streaming partners via our API. Listeners hear your story before your track plays.

Questions

Is it free? ▼
Yes. Recording and distributing stories is free during our early access period. We believe in proving value before charging for it.
Which streaming platforms do you distribute to? ▼
We're currently in partnership discussions with Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, and YouTube Music. Stories will be delivered to all connected platforms as partnerships go live.
How long should my story be? ▼
Stories can be anywhere from 5 seconds to 5 minutes. Most artists find 30 seconds to 2 minutes hits the sweet spot — enough to share the inspiration without losing the listener.
Do I own my stories? ▼
Your stories are your content. You grant Storyteller a license to distribute them to streaming platforms on your behalf. You can update, replace, or remove a story at any time.
What if I already have recorded stories elsewhere? ▼
You can upload pre-recorded audio files in MP3, WAV, AAC, or WebM format. No need to re-record — just upload what you have.
I'm a label manager. Can I manage multiple artists? ▼
Yes. Storyteller supports multi-label, multi-artist workflows from day one. Label managers can invite artists, manage catalogs, and oversee stories across their roster.

# Storyteller — Partner Meeting Agenda

## Pre-meeting
Review the Production Plan PDF before the meeting so both parties have the same baseline.

---

## 1. Align on the vision (10 min)

Walk through the core concept together. Make sure you're both describing the same thing:
- Storyteller is a **story distribution platform**, not a streaming app
- Artists record stories → Storyteller distributes → streaming platforms play them
- We slot into the existing music distribution ecosystem (like DistroKid for stories)

**Key question:** Are we aligned that this is a distribution platform, not a consumer app?

---

## 2. v1 scope — what's in, what's out (20 min)

Review the v1 feature list from the production plan. The current v1 includes:
- Artist portal (web + mobile) for recording/uploading stories
- Story management backend with verification workflow
- Distribution API for streaming platforms
- Admin/moderation tooling

The following are **deferred but architecturally supported:**
- AI narrative generation / voice likeness
- Archival compilation (interview assembly)
- Lyrics validation engine
- Instrumental crossfade
- Analytics dashboard
- Interactive storytelling

**Key questions:**
- Are there deferred features either of you feel strongly should be in v1?
- Are there v1 features that feel unnecessary for launch?
- What's the minimum we need to demo to a streaming platform?

---

## 3. Legal structure (15 min)

This needs to happen before spending money on development.

**Decisions needed:**
- [ ] Form an LLC? Where to incorporate?
- [ ] Ownership split between David and Robert
- [ ] Who has decision-making authority on what? (product, business, technical)
- [ ] Is the label partner involved in ownership, or just an early customer?
- [ ] Budget for a business attorney to draft the operating agreement?

---

## 4. Patent filing (10 min)

The provisional patent draft is ready. Filing costs $75–150 and gives 12 months of protection.

**Decisions needed:**
- [ ] File now or wait? (Recommendation: file ASAP to lock in priority date)
- [ ] Who handles the filing? Self-file or hire a patent attorney for review first?
- [ ] Budget for a patent attorney if you want professional review ($2k–5k for provisional review)

---

## 5. Artist rights & content ownership (15 min)

When an artist uploads a story, the legal relationship needs to be clear.

**Decisions needed:**
- [ ] Who owns the story content? (Artist retains ownership, grants Storyteller distribution license?)
- [ ] Can Storyteller distribute to any platform, or does the artist choose?
- [ ] Can an artist revoke/pull a story after it's been distributed?
- [ ] What about AI voice likeness consent for future features?
- [ ] Do you need a lawyer to draft these terms, or start with a simple agreement?

---

## 6. First artists & label partnership (10 min)

The label partner is the early adopter advantage. Define the relationship.

**Decisions needed:**
- [ ] How many artists can the label bring for initial launch? (Target: enough for 20–50 songs)
- [ ] Is the label a paying customer, a free early adopter, or an equity partner?
- [ ] What does the label need from Storyteller to get artists onboarded? (Invites, training, support?)
- [ ] Timeline: when could artists realistically start recording stories?

---

## 7. Budget & funding (15 min)

Be honest about what you're willing to invest.

**Development cost range:**
- Freelance (international): $20k–40k
- Freelance (US senior): $40k–80k
- Agency: $50k–120k

**Infrastructure:** ~$50/month to start

**Other costs:**
- Patent filing: $75–150 (provisional)
- LLC formation: $100–500 depending on state
- Attorney (optional): $2k–5k for patent review, $1k–3k for operating agreement
- Domain/branding: $100–500

**Decisions needed:**
- [ ] What's the total budget you're both comfortable with?
- [ ] How is funding split between partners?
- [ ] Self-fund, or raise money? (Recommendation: self-fund v1, raise after you have a working product and artist traction)

---

## 8. Hiring approach (10 min)

**Decisions needed:**
- [ ] Freelancers, agency, or equity developers?
- [ ] Where to find them? (Upwork, Toptal, referrals, dev agency)
- [ ] Who manages the developers? (David, Robert, or both?)
- [ ] Is Claude Code building the backend? (Can reduce dev hiring needs significantly)

---
